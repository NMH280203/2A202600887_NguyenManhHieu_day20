### Proposed Paper Title
"AI Agents for Research Assistance: Capabilities, Evaluation, and Open Problems"

### Draft Abstract
This survey paper explores the emerging role of AI agents in research assistance, focusing on their capabilities, evaluation methodologies, and the open problems that remain in the field. We analyze the current landscape of AI agents, highlighting their strengths in automating repetitive tasks and generating insights from large datasets. We also discuss various evaluation frameworks and strategies that assess the performance of these agents in real-world research contexts. Furthermore, we identify critical gaps in the evaluation of AI agents and propose a comprehensive benchmark that should measure their effectiveness across multiple dimensions. This paper aims to provide a foundational understanding for researchers and practitioners interested in leveraging AI agents for enhanced research productivity.

### Section-by-Section Outline

1. **Introduction**
   - **Purpose**: To introduce the topic and significance of AI agents in research.
   - **Key Themes**: Definition of AI agents, their relevance in research, and the scope of the survey.
   - **Questions**: What are AI agents? Why are they important for research assistance?
   - **Likely Pitfalls**: Overgeneralization of AI capabilities; neglecting limitations.
   - **Open Problems**: Lack of consensus on definitions and classifications of AI agents.

2. **Capabilities of AI Agents**
   - **Purpose**: To examine the functionalities and strengths of AI agents in research.
   - **Key Themes**: Automation of tasks, data analysis, literature review, and hypothesis generation.
   - **Questions**: What tasks can AI agents perform effectively? Where do they excel compared to human researchers?
   - **Likely Pitfalls**: Focusing only on successful applications without addressing limitations.
   - **Open Problems**: Identifying tasks where AI agents struggle, particularly in creative and critical thinking.

3. **Evaluation Frameworks for AI Agents**
   - **Purpose**: To discuss various evaluation methodologies for assessing AI agents.
   - **Key Themes**: Performance metrics, qualitative vs. quantitative evaluations, and user satisfaction.
   - **Questions**: What frameworks exist for evaluating AI agents? How do they differ?
   - **Likely Pitfalls**: Inconsistent application of evaluation metrics across studies.
   - **Open Problems**: Need for standardized evaluation benchmarks that encompass diverse research tasks.

4. **Case Studies of AI Agents in Research**
   - **Purpose**: To provide real-world examples of AI agents applied in research settings.
   - **Key Themes**: Success stories, challenges faced, and lessons learned.
   - **Questions**: What are notable case studies? What insights can be drawn from these applications?
   - **Likely Pitfalls**: Cherry-picking successful cases without considering failures.
   - **Open Problems**: Documentation of failures and their implications for future research.

5. **Challenges and Limitations of AI Agents**
   - **Purpose**: To identify the inherent challenges and limitations faced by AI agents.
   - **Key Themes**: Ethical concerns, biases in AI, and limitations in understanding context.
   - **Questions**: What are the primary challenges in deploying AI agents? How do biases affect their performance?
   - **Likely Pitfalls**: Underestimating the impact of ethical considerations.
   - **Open Problems**: Developing frameworks to mitigate bias and enhance ethical AI use.

6. **Future Directions in AI Research Assistance**
   - **Purpose**: To explore potential advancements and future research areas for AI agents.
   - **Key Themes**: Integration with human researchers, advancements in AI technology, and interdisciplinary applications.
   - **Questions**: What future developments are anticipated? How can AI agents evolve to better assist researchers?
   - **Likely Pitfalls**: Speculating without grounding in current trends.
   - **Open Problems**: Identifying interdisciplinary applications that could benefit from AI agents.

7. **Evaluation Gaps in Current Research**
   - **Purpose**: To highlight the most critical gaps in the evaluation of AI agents.
   - **Key Themes**: Lack of comprehensive metrics, insufficient user feedback mechanisms, and variability in task performance.
   - **Questions**: What are the main gaps in current evaluation practices? How do these gaps affect the reliability of AI agents?
   - **Likely Pitfalls**: Failing to recognize the importance of user-centered evaluations.
   - **Open Problems**: Establishing a unified framework for evaluating AI agents across different research domains.

8. **Proposed Benchmark for AI Agents**
   - **Purpose**: To define what a strong future benchmark should measure for AI agents.
   - **Key Themes**: Performance metrics, user interaction quality, adaptability, and contextual understanding.
   - **Questions**: What dimensions should a benchmark cover? How can it be implemented effectively?
   - **Likely Pitfalls**: Creating overly complex benchmarks that are difficult to apply.
   - **Open Problems**: Balancing comprehensiveness with practicality in benchmark design.

- **Key Claims**: A strong benchmark should encompass multiple dimensions of AI agent performance, including accuracy, user satisfaction, and adaptability to various research tasks [1][3].
- **Comparison of Viewpoints**: There is debate on whether benchmarks should prioritize technical performance metrics or user experience metrics, indicating a need for a balanced approach [4][5].
